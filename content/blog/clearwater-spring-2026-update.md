Heading into spring, the Clearwater-area market is sending mixed signals — and that's actually useful information. Single-family homes in walkable areas are moving briskly. Older waterfront condos are not. Knowing which side of that line your property sits on is most of the conversation right now.

## By the Numbers

A snapshot of what I'm seeing across Pinellas County so far this quarter:

- Median single-family days-on-market: roughly 32 days, down from 45 last fall.
- Median single-family sale-to-list ratio: ~97%, with well-prepared listings frequently going at or above ask.
- Condo inventory: up significantly year-over-year, particularly for buildings affected by milestone inspection requirements.
- Cash buyer share: still elevated relative to national averages — roughly one in three transactions.

## What Buyers Should Watch

If you're shopping single-family in a desirable submarket — Safety Harbor, Dunedin, parts of Clearwater proper, Palm Harbor — be ready to move on the right home within a week. If you're shopping condos, the leverage has shifted. Ask hard questions about reserves, milestone inspection status, and special assessments. A great-looking listing price can hide a $40,000 surprise on the next association meeting agenda.

## What Sellers Should Know

Single-family sellers in good condition and the right price band can list with real confidence. Condo sellers face a more delicate task — pricing has to reflect not just comparable sales but the specific condition and financial health of your building. The same two-bedroom unit in two different buildings can transact thirty percent apart based on association factors alone.

> The spread between best-prepared listings and the rest has never been wider here. Preparation isn't a nice-to-have anymore.

## The Out-of-State Buyer Effect

Roughly 40% of my Clearwater buyers this quarter are coming from outside Florida — heavily from the Midwest, with a steady flow from the Northeast. They're more deliberate than the wave we saw in 2021 and 2022, doing more upfront diligence on insurance, flood zones, and HOA health. That's good for the market's long-term stability.
