I have a very specific specialty: I help Chicago-area families relocate to Florida's Gulf Coast. I sell in both markets, and I've made the same drive my clients are about to make more times than I can count. If you're seriously considering the Clearwater area as your next chapter, here's the unfiltered version of what I tell my clients over coffee.

## First: Be Honest About the Why

The strongest moves are clear about their motivation. Lower taxes? Lifestyle? Closer to grandkids? Escaping winter? Each answer leads to a different shortlist. Buyers who can name the why in one sentence end up in the right home far faster than buyers who just want 'something warm'.

## The Tax Picture — Realistically

Yes, Florida has no state income tax. But the savings depend entirely on your income profile. If you're a high-earning W-2 household or have meaningful retirement and investment income, the savings are real and immediate. If most of your income was already shielded, the move is more lifestyle than tax play. Property taxes in Pinellas tend to run 1.0%–1.3% of assessed value — meaningfully lower than Cook County, but not nothing. Insurance and HOA fees often make up some of the difference.

## Schools: Different, Not Worse

If you're moving from a Park Ridge, Glenview, or Wilmette district, you'll want to do your homework. Pinellas County has strong magnet, IB, and choice options, and several A-rated zoned schools, but the simple address-equals-school logic of the North Shore doesn't apply the same way. Plan a school day visit. Don't choose a neighborhood for the beach alone.

## Lifestyle: The Real Adjustment

The first six months are the honeymoon. Months six through eighteen are when the move actually settles in. My clients consistently underestimate three things:

- Summer humidity. June through September is real — you'll spend more time inside than you think.
- Hurricane prep. Most years are uneventful; one year you'll be very glad you have a plan.
- Distance from old friends. The first year of holidays is the hardest. The homes that work best have a great guest room.

## The Two-Home Question

A growing number of my clients don't pick — they keep a smaller Chicago condo or townhouse and a Gulf Coast home, splitting the year. With remote and hybrid work normalized, this is more viable than ever. The key is choosing two properties that genuinely fit how you'll live, not two compromises stitched together.

> The best Florida move I've seen wasn't the boldest one. It was the most honest one — about money, about climate, and about what they'd actually miss.

## A Sample Two-Trip Plan

Most of my Chicago-to-Clearwater clients land on a similar process: a long-weekend scouting trip to walk three or four neighborhoods, then a focused buying trip two to six weeks later with five to eight homes to see in two days. We pre-screen aggressively by video so the in-person time is spent on real contenders. This approach saves trips, money, and decision fatigue.

If you're starting to think seriously about a Gulf Coast move, the best first step is usually a 30-minute conversation. I can give you a candid read on what your Chicago home is worth today, what the same money buys in Clearwater, and whether the timing makes sense for your specific situation.
