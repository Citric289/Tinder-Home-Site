Every spring, buyers and sellers ask me a version of the same question: is this a good time to make a move? After twenty-five years in this market — and nearly 150 transactions in Park Ridge alone — my answer has gotten more precise, not less. The short version for 2026: inventory is finally returning to something closer to normal, rates have stabilized in the low-to-mid sixes, and the buyers who waited out 2024 are quietly coming back.

## Where Inventory Stands

Across the greater Chicago metro, active listings are up roughly 18% year-over-year. That sounds dramatic, but it's catching up from a historically thin floor. In the most desirable pockets — Park Ridge, Glenview, Evanston, Hinsdale — well-priced homes still go in days, sometimes hours. The difference now is that buyers have a second and third option to consider, which has taken some of the heat out of escalation clauses.

## Rates: Stop Waiting for a Miracle

Thirty-year fixed mortgages have hovered between 6.25% and 6.75% for nearly nine months. Most of my buyers who planned their purchase around a return to 4% have given up that strategy — and rightly so. If you can comfortably afford a payment at today's rate, waiting another year to save a quarter-point usually costs you more in home appreciation than you'd save on interest.

> Marry the house, date the rate. Refinancing is a paperwork problem. Missing the right home is a regret problem.

## What This Means for Sellers

If you've been sitting on a home you've outgrown, the spring 2026 market rewards preparation more than urgency. The days of listing on a Thursday and accepting fifteen offers by Sunday have softened. Pricing accurately on day one matters more than ever, because buyers and their agents are watching cumulative days on market more closely than they did two years ago.

Practical things that move the needle right now:

- Professional pre-listing photos and a tight written narrative — buyers shop online for hours before they ever schedule a showing.
- Strategic pre-inspection on homes over twenty years old. Surprises kill deals; disclosed issues rarely do.
- Realistic pricing tied to the last sixty days of comparable sales, not last spring's peak.

## What This Means for Buyers

You finally have room to think. Make a list of non-negotiables — schools, commute, lot size, layout — and write down what you're willing to flex on. The buyers who win in this market are the ones who know their answer before they walk in the door. Get pre-approved with a local lender, not just pre-qualified online. When a Park Ridge home in the right school district comes up underpriced, you have hours to act, not days.

## The Park Ridge Picture

Park Ridge specifically continues to outperform the broader Chicago metro. Median sale prices are up roughly 4% year-over-year, days-on-market are tracking under three weeks for well-prepared listings, and inventory remains tight in the $600K–$900K band where demand is heaviest. If you're considering selling here, the question isn't whether the market is good — it's whether your home is presented to compete.
